Procedural / Technique

IR — Interventional Radiology
DSA — Digital Subtraction Angiography (the imaging technique for vascular studies)
EGD — Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (upper endoscopy)
MRA — Magnetic Resonance Angiography
EBL — Estimated Blood Loss
MAP — Mean Arterial Pressure

Anatomy / Vessels

GDA — Gastroduodenal Artery
RAS — Renal Artery Stenosis
AV shunting — Arteriovenous shunting (abnormal arterial-to-venous flow, common in tumors)
Arc of Bueler (actually Arc of Bühler) — an anatomic variant collateral between the celiac and SMA

Pathology / Clinical

HCC — Hepatocellular Carcinoma
UGI bleed — Upper Gastrointestinal bleed
AKI — Acute Kidney Injury
ESRD — End-Stage Renal Disease (spelled out in the report, but a common one)

Dose / Measurement Units

mGy — milligray (radiation dose)
cGycm² — centigray·cm² (dose-area product, fluoroscopy exposure metric)
mmHg — millimeters of mercury (pressure)
French / F (as in "5 French sheath") — catheter sizing unit (1 Fr ≈ 0.33 mm outer diameter)

Devices / Equipment (brand/jargon, not true abbreviations but domain-specific)

Simmons 1 / Cobra 2 / C2 — shaped selective angiographic catheters
Renegade STC, Transcend, Fathom, Glidewire, Bentson — microcatheter/guidewire product names
IDC coils, Tornado coils, Interlock coils — embolization coil types
Angio-Seal / Mynx — arteriotomy vascular closure devices
Omnipaque 300 / Visipaque 320 — iodinated contrast agents (number = iodine concentration in mg/mL)

Segment notation

"Segment 5," "Segment 8," "Segment 6" — refers to the Couinaud liver segmentation system used to localize hepatic lesions; these also should be expanded to appear as laymen friendly
