How to Use AngioVision

Ask questions about your imaging database in plain English — no SQL and no coding required. An AI assistant translates your question, runs it safely against the data, and explains the result. Here is everything you need, in about two minutes.

Quick Start

// three steps to your first answer

1

Ask a question

Click the box at the bottom of the screen and type what you want to know, for example “How many patients are in the database?” Press Enter to send (Shift+Enter for a new line).

2

Let the assistant work

AngioVision plans the query, runs it against the database, and automatically retries if something does not work. A live status shows each step as it happens.

3

Read and explore

You get a clear, written answer. Switch the tabs on the result to see a chart, the exact SQL, the raw table, or every step the assistant took.

What You Can Ask

// four kinds of questions

DICOM metadata

Counts and details about patients, studies, series, instances, modalities, equipment, dates, and radiation dose.

How many studies were performed each year?

Radiology reports

Search the free-text radiology reports — findings, signing physician, impressions — and link them back to the matching studies.

Find all studies whose report mentions stenosis.

Image similarity

Attach an angiography frame and find the most visually similar cases using AI image search, each enriched with its report.

Show me the 5 most similar sequences to this image.

Charts & graphs

Ask for a plot and the assistant fetches the numbers and draws it right in the browser — bar, line, or pie.

Plot a bar chart of the number of studies per year.

Not sure where to start? Click any of the suggestion chips just above the input box to drop a ready-made question straight into the prompt, then press Enter.

Understanding Your Results

// the tabs on every answer

Answer

A plain-language summary of what the data shows. This is what you will read most of the time.

Chart

Appears when you ask for a visual — a bar, line, or pie chart drawn from the results.

SQL

The exact database query that ran, with a one-click copy button if you want to reuse it.

Table

The raw rows returned by the query, so you can inspect the underlying data yourself.

Steps

Every action the assistant took, in order — handy for checking its reasoning or troubleshooting.

Handy Controls

// make the most of it

Suggestion chips. Click a chip above the input to auto-fill an example question.
Choose a model. Pick one in the top-right. Larger models handle harder questions; smaller ones are faster.
Thinking mode. Toggle it in the sidebar to let the model reason before answering — better for complex questions.
Attach an image. Use the paperclip button to upload an angiography frame for visual similarity search.
Stop anytime. Press the stop button to cancel a query that is still running.
History. Your previous questions are saved in the sidebar so you can revisit them.
Light or dark. Switch themes with the toggle in the header — your choice is remembered.

Your data stays private. Every query, image search, and answer is processed locally on your institution’s own server — no images or patient information ever leave the premises.
